However as I mentioned, there is another way to test this reworked part
(U11) without firmware, though it is not quite as neat!

You can manually enable the power supply (U11) by applying 3.3V to its
enable pin as shown below. In this case, you will need to either use an
external 3.3V supply (with a common ground with the board) or use the
board's own 3.3V supply (the easiest place to find this is the left side of
C81).

[image: image.png]


Therefore the test sequence is:

1. Connect a USB cable to the board's Micro USB connector to power it
2. Temporarily connect 3.3V from either an external supply or the left
side of C81 to the bottom of the resistor highlighted above (next to the
text 'R51')
3. Measure the voltage on the right side of C52 or C88, which are just
below U11
4. If this voltage is ~3.6V then the board is fine

If the above is not something you are able to do then we will have to go
with 'plan B' and ask you to modify 10 boards and return them to Spacelab
for testing.
